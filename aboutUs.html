Formosa Fabella Naturae

Formosa Fabella Naturae means “A beautiful little story of nature.”

Our name is inspired by the quiet, meaningful moments found in the wilderness—stories shared around campfires, the hush of dawn in the forest, and the timeless bond between people and the natural world.

Formosa is a first-declension adjective in the nominative feminine singular, meaning “beautiful.”

Fabella is a first-declension noun in the nominative case, meaning “a little story” or “a short tale.”

It serves as the subject of the phrase.

Naturae is a first-declension noun in the genitive case, meaning “of nature.”

It expresses possession—this is a story belonging to nature itself.

Together, the phrase evokes a poetic image: each product we offer is part of a larger narrative—crafted

for those who seek beauty, simplicity, and meaning in the wild.

We believe that every journey into nature is a chapter, and every piece of gear should be worthy of the tale it helps tell.
